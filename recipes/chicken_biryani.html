Chicken Biryani

Description

Pakistani-style Chicken Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish made by layering spiced chicken with aromatic basmati rice. It's a perfect combination of juicy, marinated chicken and fluffy, fragrant rice, often served on special occasions and family gatherings. Infused with spices, herbs, and saffron, this dish offers a rich blend of textures and flavors.

The recipe begins by marinating chicken in a mixture of yogurt, spices, and herbs, which enhances its flavor and tenderness. Separately, basmati rice is partially cooked with whole spices like cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon, infusing it with a delicate aroma. The marinated chicken is then cooked with onions, tomatoes, and green chilies until tender, creating a rich, flavorful gravy. The final step involves layering the cooked chicken and rice in a pot, topped with fresh herbs, fried onions, and saffron-infused milk. The biryani is then slow-cooked on low heat (dum style), allowing the flavors to meld together and the rice to absorb the spices, resulting in a deliciously fragrant and perfectly spiced dish.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice:

  • 3 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 4-5 green cardamoms
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste

For the Biryani:

  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 3-4 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (whole)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron (soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • 3-4 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • Fried onions for garnishing
  • Lemon slices (optional)

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken:

    • In a large bowl, mix together the yogurt, ginger paste, garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, cumin powder, coriander powder, green chilies, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.
    • Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, coat them well, and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (you can marinate for up to 2 hours for better flavor).

  2. Cook the Rice:

    • In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add green cardamoms, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves, cumin seeds, and salt.
    • Add the soaked basmati rice and cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked (the grains should still have a bite to them).
    • Drain the rice and set it aside.

  3. Cook the Chicken:

    • In a large pan, heat the oil or ghee. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
    • Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken changes color and the masala releases oil.
    • Add chopped tomatoes, cumin seeds, biryani masala, and whole green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the chicken is cooked through (about 10-15 minutes).

  4. Layer the Biryani:

    • In a large pot, start layering the biryani. First, add a layer of cooked chicken, then a layer of rice. Sprinkle chopped coriander, mint leaves, and fried onions over the rice.
    • Drizzle the saffron milk on top and add a few lemon slices if desired.
    • Repeat the layering process if you have more chicken and rice.

  5. Dum Cooking:

    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If your lid isn't tight enough, place a clean kitchen towel over the pot before covering with the lid.
    • Cook on low heat (you can also place a griddle or tawa under the pot) for 20-25 minutes. This step allows the flavors to meld together.

  6. Serve:

    • Once done, gently mix the layers of chicken and rice before serving.
    • Garnish with fried onions and fresh coriander.
